M51/NGC 5195:

  • Other designations: Messier 51b, UGC 8494, PGC 47413, Arp 85
  • Summary:
  • Dwarf galaxy interacting with the Whirlpool Galaxy (aka M51a or NGC 5194
  • Both galaxies are located ~25 million light-years away
  • In the constellation Canes Venatici
  • One of the most famous interacting galaxy pairs
  • Characteristics/observation data:
  • Constellation:        Canes Venatici
  • Right ascension: 13h 29m 59.6s
  • Declination: +47° 15′ 58″
  • Redshift: 465 ± 10 km/s
  • Distance: 25 ± 3 Mly or 7.7 ± 1.0 Mpc
  • Apparent magnitude:        10.5
  • Type: amorphous or SB0 pec
  • Apparent size:        5′.8 × 4′.6
  • Research:
  • History:
  • [20 March 1781] discovered by Pierre Méchain
  • Interaction with the Whirlpool Galaxy:
  • Together, they comprise one of the most noted interacting galaxy pairs
  • 2 galaxies are listed in the Atlas of Peculiar Galaxies as one of several prominent examples of a spiral galaxy with a companion galaxy
  • System was also the subject of very early theoretical investigations into galaxy interactions
  • Connected by a dust-rich tidal bridge
  • Dust in this tidal bridge can be seen silhouetted against the center of NGC 5195
  • Demonstrates that NGC 5195 appears to lie behind the Whirlpool Galaxy
  • Encounter has significantly enhanced the spiral structure of M51
  • [January 2016] BBC science reporter Jonathan Webb 
  • Astronomers have spotted 2 huge waves of gas being burped by the black hole at the heart of a nearby galaxy
  • Swathes of hot gas (detected in X-ray images from NASA's Chandra space telescope) appear to be sweeping cooler hydrogen gas ahead of them
  • Vast, rippling belch is taking place in NGC 5195 (a small, neglected sibling of the Whirlpool Galaxy, 26 million light-years away)
  • Makes it one of the closest black holes blasting gas in this way
  • Findings were presented at the 227th meeting of the American Astronomical Society (AAS) in Florida
  • Dramatic example of feedback between a supermassive black hole and its host galaxy
  • Webb's report cited Marie Machacek, co-author of the study from the Harvard–Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics (CFA)
  • Thinks feedback keeps galaxies from becoming too large
  • Can also be responsible for how some stars form
  • Black holes can create, not just destroy
  • Morphology:
  • As a consequence of the gravitational interaction with the Whirlpool Galaxy, NGC 5195 is highly distorted
  • Classification of its morphology is difficult
  • Sometimes identified as a lenticular galaxy or as an amorphous/irregular galaxy
  • Falls outside the standard morphological classification system
  • Supernova:
  • Supernova 1945A is the only supernova detected within NGC 5195
  • [6 April 1945] supernova was found 10″ NW of the nucleus by Milton L. Humason using the 100-inch (2.5 m) telescope at Mount Wilson Observatory
  • Type I supernova
  • Reached a peak apparent magnitude of 14.0
